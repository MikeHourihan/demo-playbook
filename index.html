Before the call

Five minutes of prep is the difference between generic and specific.

Their website, 2 minutes

Scan the services menu, location, any named physicians, any visible tech (vizia, Podium, Growth99, aura, Zenoti). Find one specific detail you can reference in the first three minutes.

Their socials, 1 minute

Instagram. Posting style, before-and-afters, follower count, what they emphasize. You’re not researching to pitch them back at themselves, you’re researching to understand their voice before you talk.

Existing RepeatMD customer? Pull the dashboard

Revenue, member count, transaction volume. You should be able to cite a specific number. The dashboard is the fastest way to prove you did the work.

“I’m looking at your dashboard and you’re doing about 5K a month in membership revenue. That’s solid.”

Identify the prospect

Before the call starts, make a guess: existing RMD customer, cold practice owner, values-forward MD-led, channel partner, or pre-sold warm inbound. See the Discovery tab for the archetype map. Your opener changes based on which of these you’re talking to.

One specific opener tied to the research

Not flattery, not a rehearsed line. Something from your research that lets the prospect teach you something.

“I was looking at your site, you’ve got a pretty complete menu. I’m curious about your Moxie laser program specifically.”

“I see you’ve got vizia in practice. How’s that working for you?”

The edge: prep is the clearest signal you did the work. It buys you permission to get specific in discovery.

3

The demo

Pause and reframe. Do not tour-guide.

Walking the assessment
Concern tiles, tailored to them

As you walk the concern tiles, plant the not-generic point early.

“Notice it only pulls the concerns and treatments you actually offer. You set that up on the back end, so everything they see is tailored to your practice.”

This is the first not-generic seed. You water it again at the reveal.

Lead-capture gateS4g

“Before they see the reveal, it asks for their info. This is the moment you capture the lead.”

“There’s a box with a mystery question mark. Your beauty score is ready, you’re ahead of most people in your age group, but there’s one factor holding you back.”

That teaser is the hook. It is what makes them hand over real contact info to see the rest.

“Two-factor, so no bots, no fake numbers. These are real people.”

The 15-second load

From submit to reveal there’s about fifteen seconds of generation. Fill it. Do not sit silent, do not ramble.

“Right now it’s doing two things. Preparing the transformation based on everything they just told us, and generating a full assessment of where they are today, with the insights behind it.”

A steady voiceover here beats a silent progress bar.

Beauty score, a must-sayO2

Four things to land, and say the last one unprompted.

“Scores land in the high 70s to high 80s. It never hands anyone a bad grade, and it’s relative to their age, we’re not rating a 25-year-old against a 70-year-old.”

“This is also how it goes viral. They compare with friends, they share it.”

“Some people feel it’s a little objectifying, and you can shut it off entirely.”

Say the shut-off unprompted. It is the hidden objection they will never raise out loud but will quietly hold against you.

The reveal, the hinge
THE REVEALS4j

“This is the conversion moment. This is where they stop in their tracks and book.”

Watch their face, not the screen. Click off the screen share so you can see them. It is about 50/50, half light up, half stay flat, and if you miss the flat reaction and keep going, you have lost them.

If they light up, ride it straight into booking. If they don’t, work the branch below.

If they don’t light up

Do not pretend you didn’t see the squint. Lean in.

“What do you think? What are you seeing here?”

Get them talking, then reframe it as the whole point.

“This is the conversation you want to have. The AI is smart, but I’m smarter, I can see what it’s trying to do. If this is the goal, let’s talk about how I’d actually get you there.”

That is the break between a clinical horror tool and ageless. You are not handing them a diagnosis, you are starting a conversation.

Bring it back to their concerns

The flat reaction is usually a silent fear: I can’t deliver that, and they’ll be mad. Defuse it.

“Remember the concerns you flagged, the under-eye, the jowls. This isn’t generic imaging, it’s built from what you told me, tuned to a natural look.”

“And see the description, it says treatments over time, two weeks out. This isn’t one visit, it’s the plan.”

Treatments-over-time is what wins back the they-won’t-walk-in-looking-like-that worry.

Save and refer

At the before-and-after, point out the two buttons. This is a viral and a validation moment.

“They can save this look and send it to a friend. That’s what people do, they text it to their husband, see, this is what I’d look like, and now he’s validating the purchase for you.”

Save and refer spreads your brand and closes the loop at home. Most reps skip it.

When the objections hit
You’re the expertA3

“The world still needs doctors, and that’s a good thing. You’re still the intervention layer.”

“You pit yourself against the AI: I see what it’s doing here, but when I look at your face, here’s how I’d really do it.”

Accuracy bundleO3

“It’s an AI simulation, it says so right on the screen. Like McDonald’s telling you the coffee’s hot. We don’t have a time machine in the back.”

“The board scrutinized these images for months. We put them through the ringer, Tinder-style swipes, laser pointers in a room arguing over a single result. And you’re still the expert who tells them how you’d really do it.”

This is where the board detail pays off. You set the table on slide 7, you spend it here.

Can they just do it themselves?

“Sure, they could do this on their own. But it’s not tied to your treatments, it’s a generic catalog. In here it’s pulled from what you actually offer, and it recommends you.”

Yes-and it. Don’t fight the premise, redirect to what only you have.

They’ll Google a med spa insteadA4

“They can do this on ChatGPT, and you don’t want that. Because the next thing they do is search find a med spa near me, and you may not be on that list. Better they do it with you.”

Conversion rate, by distribution

When they ask the conversion rate, don’t give one number. Tie it to where they put it, it doubles as coaching them to distribute.

“It’s not one number, it depends where you put it. A QR in the waiting room or an email to your VIP list converts over 50%. A Facebook ad into your site, more like 20 to 30%. So the more places you run it, the better the whole thing performs.”

This is a buyer question. Lean in, give the ranges, and use it to bake in put-it-everywhere.

Then, the emotional close
Future self, the secret weaponS4l

“Gravity is everybody’s enemy, and it catches everyone. This is why you keep coming back to me.”

“People go, oh my God, I look like my mother. That’s the moment they book, and the moment they text it to a friend.”

Don’t make it only for older patients. It hits younger ones harder, the ones who think they’ll never age, until they see themselves in fifteen years.

“And drop in a personal story: I saw a photo of myself from a few years back and thought, I really had it together, I need to get back on that. That lands.”

Past-self and Tommy

“On wellness, flip it. This isn’t what you could look like, it’s what you used to look like.”

“This is Tommy before he got married. That’s a bigger motivator than what you could look like.”

Wellness, demystify the names

On the wellness side, the win is taking the intimidation out of the names.

“You see something like CJC-1295 and think, what is that, what are all these chemicals? Here we break down what it is, what it does to your body, and how it works.”

People hear peptides and don’t know where to start. Showing what-it-is and what-it-does is the moment they decide to try it.

Only recommends what you offer

“It only ever recommends what you offer, because you set it up on the back end.”

Thread this every few beats. It is the quiet worry under the whole demo.

The edge: the whole demo is reading the silent objection and leaning in, not plowing past it. The two-up rep does the reframes, the solo rep has to do both, and has to catch the squint.

Prove the engine

“Income, homeowner status, marital status, and we never asked. It’s public record. We get it on about three of four.”

“So you can tell a fifty-year-old homeowner who wants the works from a twenty-three-year-old who wants a little baby Botox.”

Marketing hub, aesthetic-labs

“We built a fake company called Aesthetic Labs, spent real money running ads to find the winners, and we hand you the winners. They’re already tested in the wild.”

Show the marketing hub in Amora.

One more thing to manage?

“If it ain’t broke, don’t fix it. Keep your follow-up where it is, we just feed it.”

“I don’t want to order a pizza. Someone’s going to pick up the phone and call you, don’t stop that.”

“If setup looks like a lot, screenshot it, drop it into your AI, and it’ll walk you through it in five minutes.”

Frame the value
What’s one patient worthC4Slide 15

“What’s one patient worth to you, more or less than 499?”

Get them to say the number first. Then show them the math.

The volume mathSlide 16

“If all ageless did was bring you three new patients a month at a thousand each, that’s thirty-six thousand a year against about six grand in cost. A 6x return, and that’s the floor.”

Keep it conservative on purpose. A thousand is a baseline patient, and it still pays for itself many times over. This is the slide on screen, say the same numbers it shows.

Real results, who’s making moneySlide 17

The move: acknowledge the rational appeal, then hit them with real dollars.

“Sure, seeing the result before they book would be great. But here’s who’s actually making money. One practice closed a three-thousand-dollar patient before she sat down. Another sold seventy-five hundred right in the consult room. Another emailed a hundred members and booked ten thousand five hundred in a day.”

Real results land where opinion doesn’t. This goes right before the price.

Patient = one unique personSlide 19

“We say patient on purpose. One unique person, forever. Once or ten times, still one. You’re paying for an acquisition, not a click.”

The plans, anchor highSlide 19

Start on the standard plans. This is the anchor.

“Three plans. Startup is 699, Growth 899, Pro 1199, for up to 50, 100, and 150 patients a month, with a one-time 995 setup. Unused patients roll into a bank, and you can cap or upgrade anytime, so no surprise bills.”

Founders Club, the first-call discountC2Slide 21

The move: open high, then drop to Founders as the discount you give on this call.

“Get into the Founders Club today and your first month is unlimited at 499. Then it’s 499, 699, or 999 a month.”

Anchor high, drop to Founders. The leverage is that this is the first-call discount, the price on the table in the room today.

First month = volume testSlide 21

“The first month’s unlimited, so go nuts. The point isn’t that it’s cheap, it’s that you find your real volume before you pick a tier.”

Push them to do it all.

“In that first month I want you doing everything. Send the emails and texts, post it on social, add links and buttons on your treatment pages, drive ads to ageless, and use ageless in the ads themselves.”

If they doubt they’ll have 50 patients, the local-events play:

“Do you do any local events? Great. How do you run them right now?”

They’ll usually describe lugging a machine over, doing a few sample treatments, and leaving with one or two leads out of a whole room.

“So while people are waiting, you’ve got the ageless QR code. They start doing it, comparing beauty scores, showing each other their future self, the oh-my-god-I-look-like-my-mother moment. Now you leave with the whole room as leads, not two. Get good at it and you might leave the machine home and just have them try ageless and talk about it.”

Founders bonusesSlides 22-25

“And the Founders Club comes with three bonuses: a private WhatsApp community, live masterclasses with top experts, and unlimited prints for life when you join the live Welcome to Ageless call in your first five days.”

The edge: do not say a number until the engine is proven and the anchor is set.

The close

A verbal yes is not a close.

Take the card in the room

“Great. The only thing I need to get you set up is a card, and we’ll do it right now.”

A booked signup call is not a close. If the card fails, hold it as pending and run it again. Do not log it until it clears.

Park if not open yetC1

“I’ll take the card today, lock your founder price, and set your release for your launch date. You won’t pay again until a month after you go live.”

Hold price on a deal-fisherC5

“I’m not going to discount it. The price is the price.”

A clean no is a valid outcome. Be willing to walk.

Decider absent? Arm the champion

“Want me to send you the recording so you can walk them through it? Or I’ll hop on a quick call with them.”

Do not demo harder and hope. A bought-in champion beats re-pitching a cold committee.

The edge: nothing should surprise you. Discovery already told you how it ends.
