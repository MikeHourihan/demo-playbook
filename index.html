1

Discovery

Run a diagnostic, not a warmup.

Diagnostic open

“Before I show you anything, I want to understand how you get patients today, because where this fits depends on what you’re already doing.”

“What are you doing right now to get patients in the door?”

Do you want more patients?

“And now an important question. Do you want more patients?”

If not really: pivot to the other side, doing more with the patients they already have.

Stack and tools, pre-callB4

Get the stack before the call. Then do not ask, tell them.

“I see you’re using Boulevard. Here’s how this plugs right in.”

EntityMed arms A4b. A vizia or aura arms A5b. Nothing means greenfield.

Lead follow-upD1

“When a lead comes in, who follows up, and how fast?”

Discount-led answer arms A6, lead with curiosity not discount.

The four must-gets
Decider on the call?

“Who makes this decision, and is that person with us today?”

Who else decides

“Other than you, who weighs in, a partner, a numbers person, a spouse?”

Open now, or launch date

“Are you open and running now? If not, when do you launch?”

Decides close in the room versus close and park.

Their next step

“If this looks as good as I think it will, what does the next step look like on your end?”

The edge: surface the decider in minute five, not at the close.
2

Status quo + industry proof

Read their problem back so they think you get them.

Status quo, the three that fail
The read-back

“Honestly, none of what you said is a surprise. We hear the same things on every call.”

The move: name the specific things they told you in discovery. The power is the recognition, this person is reading my mind.

Discount adsA6

“Discount ads bring the wrong people, leads who won’t pay full price and don’t come back.”

Borrowed before-and-afters

“Borrowed before-and-afters are someone else’s face, so the shift never quite lands.”

“Weight Watchers has run other people’s before-and-afters since 1963, because it works. You know what works even better? Your own.”

The skin quiz

“The skin quiz collects great data but hands the patient nothing. By the time you call, they don’t remember, and the lead’s gone cold.”

Then the bridge
Brands beat it with AI

“We didn’t reinvent the wheel. The biggest brands already beat this with AI. Sephora’s try-on converts far higher, Amazon drives huge revenue off AI recommendations, leads are several times more likely to convert with fast follow-up.”

Caution: point the stats at “AI is the lever.” Never imply the brand solved their problem.

What it is

“We took that concept and built ageless. AI before-and-after imaging, AI lead qualification, and AI follow-up, built with our medical advisory board.”

What it does, the three outcomes

“And what that does for your business: acquire higher quality patients, increase revenue per visit, and reactivate existing patients.”

Social proofMello

“She went live last night, and the next day had two patients in the pipeline worth over seven thousand dollars. That’s how fast you see value.”

Advisory board, the accuracy pre-empt

“Our AI imaging is only as good as its clinical credibility, which is why we partnered with this board. Fifteen-plus months, hundreds of thousands of photos.”

“And these aren’t faces we paid to be here. We put them through the ringer, Tinder-style swipes, laser pointers in a room.”

Where patients find it

“Your website next to a treatment page, your link tree and social bios, reels and stories, ads, text and email to your base, a QR in the waiting room, events.”

The edge: perceived expert. Keep what-it-is separate from what-it-does.
3

The demo

Pause and reframe. Do not tour-guide.

Lead-capture gateS4g

“Before they see the reveal, it asks for their info. This is the moment you capture the lead.”

“Two-factor. You know what that means? No bots, no fake numbers. These are real people.”

Beauty score, a must-sayO2

“Some people think it’s a little objectifying, and you can shut it off. It never goes below a 75, we’re not handing anyone a bad grade.”

Say it unprompted. It is the hidden objection they will never raise but will hold against you.

The reveal, the hinge
THE REVEALS4j

“This is the conversion moment. This is where they stop in their tracks and book.”

Read the reaction. If they light up, ride it into booking. If they squint, draw it out: “What do you think about this? Does this look right to you?”

You’re the expertA3

“The world still needs doctors, and that’s a good thing. You’re still the intervention layer.”

“You pit yourself against the AI: I see what it’s doing here, but when I look at your face, here’s how I’d really do it.”

Accuracy bundleO3

“It’s an AI simulation, it says so right on the screen. Like McDonald’s telling you the coffee’s hot. We don’t have a time machine in the back.”

“The board scrutinized these images for months. And you’re still the expert who tells them how you’d really do it.”

They’ll Google a med spa insteadA4

“They can do this on ChatGPT, and you don’t want that. Because the next thing they do is search find a med spa near me, and you may not be on that list. Better they do it with you.”

Then
Future self, the secret weaponS4l

“Gravity is everybody’s enemy, and it catches everyone. This is why you keep coming back to me.”

“People go, oh my God, I look like my mother. That’s the moment they book, and the moment they text it to a friend.”

Past-self and Tommy

“On wellness, flip it. This isn’t what you could look like, it’s what you used to look like.”

“This is Tommy before he got married. That’s a bigger motivator than what you could look like.”

Only recommends what you offer

“It only ever recommends what you offer, because you set it up on the back end.”

Thread this every few beats. It is the quiet worry under the whole demo.

The edge: hands off the mouse, narrate meaning. The two-up rep does the reframes, the solo rep has to do both.
4

Back end, pricing, promo

Prove the engine, then gate the price behind the value.

Prove the engine
Dashboard, speed to leadD1

“Requested consults push to the top, because speed to lead is everything.”

“A lead is like McDonald’s fries. Incredible the second you get them, garbage twenty minutes later. First fifteen minutes they convert. Wait a day and you’re handing them cold, soggy fries.”

Show the home page in Meesha for the live activity feed.

Qualification scraper, the wow

“Income, homeowner status, marital status, and we never asked. It’s public record. We get it on about three of four.”

“So you can tell a fifty-year-old homeowner who wants the works from a twenty-three-year-old who wants a little baby Botox.”

Marketing hub, aesthetic-labs

“We built a fake company called Aesthetic Labs, spent real money running ads to find the winners, and we hand you the winners. They’re already tested in the wild.”

Show the marketing hub in Amora.

One more thing to manage?

“If it ain’t broke, don’t fix it. Keep your follow-up where it is, we just feed it.”

“I don’t want to order a pizza. Someone’s going to pick up the phone and call you, don’t stop that.”

“If setup looks like a lot, screenshot it, drop it into your AI, and it’ll walk you through it in five minutes.”

Frame the value
Patient = one unique person

“We say patient on purpose. One unique person, forever. Once or ten times, still one. You’re paying for an acquisition, not a click.”

Tiers, rollover, cap

“Unused patients roll into a bank. Go over, you cap it, pay ten a patient, or upgrade. No surprise bills, you control the volume.”

First month = volume test

“The first month’s unlimited, so go nuts. The point isn’t that it’s cheap, it’s that you find your real volume before you pick a tier.”

What’s one patient worthC4

“What’s one patient worth to you, more or less than 499? What if we got you five a week? And that’s just the first time they come in.”

Anchor before you quote the tier.

The edge: do not say a number until the engine is proven and the anchor is set.
5

The close

A verbal yes is not a close.

Take the card in the room

“Great. The only thing I need to get you set up is a card, and we’ll do it right now.”

A booked signup call is not a close. If the card fails, hold it as pending and run it again. Do not log it until it clears.

Park if not open yetC1

“I’ll take the card today, lock your founder price, and set your release for your launch date. You won’t pay again until a month after you go live.”

Hold price on a deal-fisherC5

“I’m not going to discount it. The price is the price.”

A clean no is a valid outcome. Be willing to walk.

Decider absent? Arm the champion

“Want me to send you the recording so you can walk them through it? Or I’ll hop on a quick call with them.”

Do not demo harder and hope. A bought-in champion beats re-pitching a cold committee.

The edge: nothing should surprise you. Discovery already told you how it ends.
